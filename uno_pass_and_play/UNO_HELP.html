UNO Rules (Reference + this HTML game)

A quick reference for how to play, plus the exact rules used by uno_pass_and_play.html.

Red Green Blue Yellow Wild +2 / +4 Skip / Reverse
Tip: In the game, use the HELP button anytime.

1) Objective

  • Be the first player to get rid of all cards in your hand.
  • This HTML version is a single-round game (no scoring mode).

2) Card Types

  • Colors: Red, Green, Blue, Yellow
  • Number cards: 0–9
  • Action cards: Skip, Reverse, Draw Two (+2)
  • Wild cards: Wild, Wild Draw Four (+4)

3) Setup

  • Shuffle the deck.
  • Deal 7 cards to each player.
  • Flip 1 card to start the discard pile; the rest is the draw pile.
  • This HTML game ensures the starting discard is a number card.

4) Turn Structure

  • Play 1 card that matches the discard (by color or symbol), or play a Wild.
  • If you cannot play, draw 1 card.
  • If the drawn card is playable, you may play it immediately; otherwise your turn ends.
  • Active color is normally the discard’s color; after a Wild, the player chooses it.

5) Matching Rules

  • Match color, or match number/symbol, or play a Wild.
  • If the top card is a Wild, the chosen active color is what you match.

6) Action & Wild Cards

  • Skip: next player loses their turn.
  • Reverse: reverses direction of play.
  • Draw Two: next player draws 2 and loses their turn.
  • Wild: choose next active color.
  • Wild Draw Four: choose next color; next player draws 4 and loses their turn.

7) Wild +4 Restriction (enabled in this game)

You may play Wild Draw Four (+4) only if you have no card in your hand matching the current active color (color match restriction).

Physical UNO often allows challenges; this HTML game enforces the restriction directly (no challenge UI).

8) Calling “UNO” (enabled in this game)

  • When you play down to exactly 1 card, you must call UNO.
  • This HTML game provides an UNO! button that becomes enabled.
  • You have 5 seconds to click it.
  • If you fail, you draw 2 cards as a penalty at the start of your next turn.

9) Winning

  • If you play your last card (hand becomes 0), you win immediately.

10) Common House Rules (not implemented here unless stated)

  • Stacking (+2 on +2 etc.): not implemented in this HTML game.
  • Draw-until-playable: varies; this HTML game generally uses single-draw then pass.
  • Jump-in (play identical out of turn): not implemented.
  • 7-0 (swap/rotate hands): not implemented.

11) How to play the included HTML pass-and-play version

  • Open uno_pass_and_play.html in a browser.
  • Choose START 4P for a normal game.
  • Choose TUTORIAL 4P for a guided demo scenario.
  • Click cards to play, click the deck or DRAW to draw, click PASS after drawing if needed.
  • When playing a Wild, pick a color on the color chooser overlay.
  • Between turns, the game shows a “Pass Device” screen for privacy.
